Lemon Sorbet

Delicious and refreshing

This recipe was originally found on BBC Good Food, though I had to increase the amount of everything's ratio to the sugar, since it turned out way too sweet when I followed the recipe. Consequently, I ended up with two tubs of lemon sorbet! No complaints here.

Let's get started

Before we begin, let me say this: Trust your own tastebuds. Add more water and lemon juice as you see fit. Also, I personally have not used any vodka in this before (Lockdown prohibition), so do let me know how it works out!

Ingredient Amount
Sugar 250g
Water 1/2 litre
Lemon Zest 2 teaspoons
Lemon Juice 10 lemons
Optional: Vodka 2 teaspoons
  1. Heat the half litre of water, the sugar and the lemon peel in a small pan until the sugar has dissolved, then bring the mixture to the boil. Cook for 3 mins, then turn off the heat and leave to cool. Pick out the lemon peel and discard. Add your lemon juice to the sugar mixture along with the vodka, if you're using some.
  2. Though pretty obvious, I found old ice cream tubs worked best as containers, though I should mention the caveat. The advantage of a smaller tub is that your lemonade will start freezing more quickly. If you use a large tub, the process of freezing and crushing ice crystals might take all day - which is what ended up happening my first time around.
  3. Pour the mix into your freezer box of choice and freeze for 1hr 30 mins. Then, mix up with a whisk to break up and incorporate the ice crystals (which will be starting to form at the edges) before returning to the freezer.
  4. Keep mixing the sorbet once an hour for between 4 to 6 hours to break up the ice crystals. I found our potato masher the best tool for this. Stop mixing when firm but still scoopable, then store in the freezer for up to 1 month.
  5. Serve scoops of sorbet decorated with a few curls of lemon zest, some leaves of mint.
  6. Go nuts.
